Given Information:
• Regression equation: ŷ = 345.45 + 6.73x
• Correlation coefficient: r = 0.989
• Sample size: n = 10
• Critical t-value at α = 0.05: ±2.306

Questions:

1. Variable Identification: Which is the independent variable and which is the dependent variable? Explain.
2. Interpret the Slope: What does the slope (7.39) mean in practical terms for the coffee shop?
3. Correlation Strength: Describe the strength and direction of the relationship with r = 0.982.
4. Test Significance: Calculate t = r√(n-2) / √(1-r²). Is the correlation significant at α = 0.05?
5. Prediction (Interpolation): Predict iced coffee sales when the temperature is 77°F.
6. Prediction (Extrapolation): Would you trust a prediction for 100°F? Why or why not?

Solutions:

1. Variables: Independent variable (x) = Temperature; Dependent variable (y) = Iced Coffee Sales. Temperature influences sales behavior, not the other way around.
2. Slope: For every 1°F increase in temperature, iced coffee sales increase by approximately $7.39. This makes business sense - warmer weather drives more cold beverage purchases.
3. Correlation: r = 0.982 indicates a very strong positive relationship. As temperature increases, iced coffee sales strongly increase.
4. Significance Test:
t = 0.982√8 / √(1-0.982²) = 0.982(2.828) / √0.0363 = 2.777 / 0.191 ≈ 14.54
Since |14.54| > 2.306, the correlation is statistically significant!
5. Interpolation: ŷ = -278.48 + 7.39(77) = -278.48 + 569.03 = $290.55 (reliable, within data range)
6. Extrapolation: ŷ = -278.48 + 7.39(100) = $460.52. This is risky because 100°F is outside our data range (65-88°F), and the linear relationship may not hold at extreme temperatures.

2. Slope Meaning: What does the slope tell us about the relationship between steps and calories?
3. Correlation Classification: With r = 0.976, classify the strength of this relationship.
4. Significance Testing: Test whether the correlation is statistically significant.

t = 0.976√8 / √(1-0.976²) = 0.976(2.828) / √0.0472 = 2.760 / 0.217 ≈ 12.72
Since 12.72 > 2.306, the correlation is highly significant!

6. Realistic Expectations: No, 25,000 steps is way beyond our data range (5,000-15,000). The linear relationship may not hold, and physical limitations could change the pattern.
