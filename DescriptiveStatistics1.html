üå± Stem-and-Leaf Plots: The Original Data Art

Stem-and-Leaf Plot: A data display that organizes numbers by splitting each value into a "stem" (leading digits) and "leaf" (trailing digit). Think of it as organized handwriting for your data!

How to Create a Stem-and-Leaf Plot:

Example: Test Scores

Raw Data: 67, 72, 75, 78, 81, 83, 84, 87, 89, 91, 93, 95

6 | 7 7 | 2 5 8 8 | 1 3 4 7 9 9 | 1 3 5 Key: 6|7 = 67

What it shows: Most students scored in the 80s, with a nice spread across the 70s and 90s!

üéØ Measures of Location: Finding the Center

Measures of Location: Statistics that describe where the "center" or "typical value" of a dataset lies. They help answer the question: "What's a representative value for this data?"

The Big Three:

1. üìä Mean (Arithmetic Average)

Formula: Sum of all values √∑ Number of values

Best for: Symmetric data without extreme outliers

Symbol: xÃÑ (x-bar) for sample, Œº (mu) for population

2. üèÉ‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏è Median (Middle Value)

Definition: The middle value when data is arranged in order

Best for: Skewed data or data with outliers

Also called: 50th percentile or Q2

3. üé™ Mode (Most Frequent)

Definition: The value that appears most often

Best for: Categorical data or finding the most common value

Types: Unimodal (one mode), bimodal (two modes), multimodal (many modes)

"Mean, median, and mode walk into a data set. The mean says 'I'm the average!' The median says 'I'm in the middle!' The mode says 'I'm popular!' üìä"

üìè Percentiles: Your Data's Report Card

Percentile: A value below which a certain percentage of data falls. The 75th percentile means 75% of the data is below this value, and 25% is above it.

Understanding Percentiles:

Test Score Example

If your test score is in the 85th percentile:

  • ‚úÖ You scored better than 85% of students
  • üìä 15% of students scored higher than you
  • üéØ You're in the top 15% of performers

üéöÔ∏è Interactive Percentile Explorer

Sample Data: Test scores from 60 to 100

Selected Percentile: 50th

Special Percentiles (Quartiles):

  • Q1 (25th percentile): First quartile - 25% below, 75% above
  • Q2 (50th percentile): Second quartile = MEDIAN - 50% below, 50% above
  • Q3 (75th percentile): Third quartile - 75% below, 25% above
  • IQR: Interquartile Range = Q3 - Q1 (middle 50% of data)
Percentiles are like a GPS for your data - they tell you exactly where you are in the distribution landscape!
