Sampling Methods: Choose Your Fighter! ‚öîÔ∏è

1. Simple Random Sampling üé≤

Everyone has an equal chance! Like pulling names from a hat.
Example: Using a random number generator to pick lottery winners

2. Stratified Sampling üìö

Divide into groups, then sample from each.
Example: Survey 10% from each grade level (freshmen, sophomores, juniors, seniors)

3. Cluster Sampling üèòÔ∏è

Pick random groups, survey everyone in those groups.
Example: Randomly select 5 classrooms, survey all students in those rooms

4. Systematic Sampling üìè

Pick every nth person.
Example: Survey every 10th person entering a mall

5. Convenience Sampling üõãÔ∏è

Use whoever's available (lazy but sometimes necessary!).
Example: Surveying your friends about pizza preferences
Most online polls use convenience sampling, which is why "Boaty McBoatface" almost became the name of a British research vessel!

Good Data Collection Practices:

Common Pitfalls to Avoid:

Response Bias: People lying about how much TV they watch üì∫
